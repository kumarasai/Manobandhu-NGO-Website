We Work To Improve The Health And Wellbeing Of Humans

These individuals, often encountered on the streets, find themselves in deeply unfortunate circumstances. They seem to be lost in their own worlds, frequently seen laughing and conversing with themselves. Their hair is long and tangled, covered in dirt, and their appearance is disheveled, with a gaunt, half-naked demeanor resembling skin and bones.

Their very survival depends on meager resources like discarded food, gutter water, and whatever scraps passersby might toss their way. Their predicament is even more dire than that of the most impoverished, for they lack anyone – we emphasize, absolutely no one – to provide them with care.

For weeks, months, or even years, they endure life on the streets without access to food, clothing, or shelter. Many people walk past them without a second thought, often failing to acknowledge their existence, as though their lives hold no significance. Stripped of all human dignity, it is vital to recognize that, despite their circumstances, they remain human beings deserving of compassion and respect.

In the year 2021, ManoBandhu was founded with the mission of addressing the heart-wrenching issue of mentally ill destitutes aimlessly wandering the streets of India.

ManoBandhu does not admit patients who are brought in by family members or whose family backgrounds are known.

ManoBandhu operates as a fully charitable, secular, social, non-political, and officially registered Non-Governmental Organization (NGO), providing all of its services completely free of charge.
